I am a social science researcher with a background in clinical psychology research and philosophy. My work is grounded in a commitment to methodological pluralism and the integration of idiographic and nomothetic approaches through both qualitative and quantitative research methods.

As such, I am passionate about mixed-methods research, and have applied interviews, focus groups, and other qualitative tools alongside classical and advanced statistical techniques to investigate complex questions in mental health and human development. I believe that, to truly apprehend the gestalt of human subjectivity and behaviour, we must pursue research that holds individual complexity and generalisable patterns in productive tension.

I am excited by opportunities—academic or commercial—that are committed to this integrative vision. I am above all interested in human meaning-making and how cultural, historical, technological, and interpersonal processes coalesce to produce emergent ways of being in the world.

My academic interests span systems theory, organisational psychology, psychotherapy research, public health, philosophy of mind, social cognition, critical psychiatry, and phenomenology. My PhD is in Clinical Psychology Research, and I hold a Research Fellowship at UCL. If you would like to contact me, please send me an e-mail at max.moser@ucl.ac.uk.
