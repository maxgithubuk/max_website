I am a mixed-methods social scientist and research fellow at UCL. I integrate data analytics with idiographic insights in order to understand people in context.

How I Can Help

Evidence Synthesis

I conduct systematic reviews, rapid evidence assessments, and meta-analyses to synthesise and summarise the current state of knowledge in a given domain. These tools enable a panoramic view of emerging trends, intervention outcomes, and conceptual developments. While individual studies and information sources are constrained by the particularities of their design and context, evidence synthesis integrates disparate findings to identify generalisable insights and elucidate complexities. This work supports proposition development, policy design, and behavioural change strategies.

Data Analytics & Segmentation

I use advanced modelling techniques, including multi-level and hierarchical modelling, latent class analysis, and segmentation to identify behavioural patterns, attitudinal clusters, and key drivers of decision-making. Whether working with survey, social, or clinical datasets, I translate data into actionable audience insights that support strategy, positioning, and messaging. My work also includes health economic modelling and outcome prediction to support evidence-led innovation in public health, policy, and service design.

Qualitative & Cultural Research

I design and analyse depth interviews, mobile ethnographies, focus groups, and cultural audits to access lived experience and meaning-making processes that structured data obscures. By using reflexive thematic and interpretive methods, I surface the narratives, contradictions, and context-specific dynamics that psychometric or behavioural data can miss—bringing complexity into focus for design, brand, and policy decision-making.

If you would like to discuss a project or collaboration, please reach out via e-mail at max.moser@ucl.ac.uk.
